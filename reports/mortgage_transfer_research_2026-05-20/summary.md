# Mainland China mortgage rate-reduction transfer strategies (public-source notes)

## Main forms seen in the market
1. **经营贷置换房贷 / 房贷转经营贷 / 转贷降息**
   - Typical pattern: use bridge funds to clear the mortgage, then re-borrow as business-purpose lending.
   - Official stance: high-risk / often违规; business-purpose funds must not be used to buy housing or repay housing-related bridge funds.

2. **消费贷 / 信用贷周转**
   - Typical pattern: short-term consumer/credit loans used to bridge repayment pressure.
   - Official stance: short tenor, easy to create debt-chain and repayment mismatch risk.

3. **赎楼 / 过桥 + 新贷**
   - Typical pattern: prepay old mortgage, release collateral, then refinance.
   - If used to disguise housing debt as other-purpose borrowing, regulators treat it as违规转贷.

4. **二抵 / 组合融资**
   - More orthodox product form: second-mortgage or mixed financing against remaining property value.
   - Legality depends on real purpose, bank approval, and not being used to secretly repay the mortgage.

## Main compliance constraints
- Purpose must match the loan contract.
- Business-purpose funds cannot flow into housing or be used to repay housing-related bridge funds.
- No fake materials, fake cashflow, or shell companies.
- Short-tenor products often have bullet principal repayment and refinancing risk.
- Bridge fees, taxes, and service charges can wipe out the headline rate advantage.
- Lenders can early-call the loan and affect credit if misuse is found.

## Common pitfalls
- Comparing only nominal interest rate.
- Treating middleman “transfer” schemes as compliant just because they can be arranged.
- Using relatives/shell entities/false materials to qualify.
- Ignoring liquidity risk when the short loan matures.
- Assuming there is a formal policy product for “转贷降息”; official docs mainly frame it as a risk to control.
